⚠ The 182-Day Tax Rule

If you stay in India for 182 or more days in a financial year (April–March), you automatically become a Resident Indian for tax purposes — even if you hold a Gulf visa. This means your worldwide income becomes taxable in India.

Track your India visits carefully. Consult a CA if your annual India stay is approaching 150+ days.

🌎 Gulf Visa Renewal — Key Facts for Keralites

✓UAE Residence Visa: Valid for 1, 2 or 3 years (or 5–10 years unsponsored). Emirates ID must match visa validity. Renewal takes 1–2 weeks — start 30 days before expiry. Source: u.ae official
✓UAE Grace Period: After residence visa cancellation or expiry, up to 6 months grace before AED 50/day fines begin. Check with ICA or GDRFA for your category. Source: u.ae official
⚠UAE 180-Day Outside Rule: Stay outside UAE for more than 180 consecutive days and your residence visa is automatically nullified. Critical for NRIs visiting India long-term. Source: u.ae official
✓Saudi Iqama: Must be renewed 3 days before expiry. Employer initiates. Check via Absher.sa app. Source: moi.gov.sa
✓Qatar RP: Tied to employment contract. Check via Metrash2 app. Exit permit abolished 2020 — you can leave freely.
⚠Overstay Fines: UAE: AED 50/day. Qatar Visit Visa: QAR 200/day. Qatar RP: QAR 10/day (max QAR 12,000). Overstay fines can block future GCC entry. Sources: u.ae, moi.gov.sa
✓Passport Validity: Indian passport must have 6+ months validity for Gulf residence visa renewal.
✓Family Visa (Dependent): If the sponsor's Iqama lapses, all dependent visas become invalid automatically.
✗India Visit During Renewal: If you are in India when your Iqama expires, you may need an Emergency Return Certificate from the Indian Embassy.
